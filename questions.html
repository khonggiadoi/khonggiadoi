By different measures of certainty, this study will assess your willingness to accept the representation of a product. In a moment, you will be asked to make judgments about various products. On the screen, you will be shown an image of a product with a particular property, by a particular brand, and in a particular context. You will then be asked to judge and rate the product in terms of surprise, ease-of-imagining, goodness-of-example, and similarity-to-an-ideal on a scale from 1 to 7.

To rate the product in terms of surprise, think: the extent to which it causes you to feel a mild astonishment; ease-of-imagining, think: the extent to which it enables you to form a mental image; goodness-of-example: the extent to which it is characteristic of its kind; and similarity-to-an-ideal, think: the extent to which it is most suitable as its kind.

For example, for the product 'suitcase' with the property 'has a zipper,' by the brand 'Samsonite,' and in the context 'at an airport,' you would be shown an image of a suitcase that has a zipper, with a Samsonite brand logo, at an airport. Then, when asked to make a judgment about surprise you would be asked, "How surprised are you to encounter an actual suitcase with a zipper by Samsonite at an airport?" You would then respond by rating your level of surprise on a scale from 1 to 7, where 1 is 'very unsurprised' and 7 is 'very surprised'.
